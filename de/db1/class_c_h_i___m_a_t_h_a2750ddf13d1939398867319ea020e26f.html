This routine creates a test matrix of a strictly diagonally dominant matrix. The matrix has the following form:

\[ A= \begin{bmatrix} k_0+k_1 & -k_1 & 0 & 0 & \cdots & 0 & 0 \\ -k_1 & k_1+k_2 & -k_2 & 0 & \cdots & 0 & 0 \\ \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots & \vdots \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & \cdots & -k_{n-1} & k_{n-1}+k_n \end{bmatrix} \ b= \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \\ \vdots \\ k_n \end{bmatrix} \]

In this form, the \(k_n\) coefficients can either be formed from a linear representation (linear=true) or a piecewise constant function. For the linear case: where \( h=\frac{1}{size+1}\) the values \( k_i = 1+(i+0.5)h \) are assigned. For the non-linear case: \( t=(i+0.5)h \) and \( k(t) = 1 \) when \( 0\le t < 0.5 \) and \( k(t)=K \) when \( 0.5\le t < 1 \). For \( K=2,100, 1000 \).
